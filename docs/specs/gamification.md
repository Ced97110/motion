## 8. Gamification and Archetypes

### 8 Player Archetypes (NO NBA PLAYER NAMES — legal compliance)

| Archetype | Defining shape | Description |
|-----------|---------------|-------------|
| Sharpshooter | High shooting, low post | Lives beyond the arc. Off-screen movement. Gravitational pull. |
| Floor General | High passing + IQ | Runs the offense. Sees two passes ahead. Coach on the floor. |
| Two-Way Wing | Balanced offense + defense | The most complete archetype. Switches 1-5. Creates both ends. |
| Athletic Slasher | High athleticism + speed | Explosive first step. Finishes through contact. Transition terror. |
| Paint Beast | High post + rebounding | Dominates interior. Blocks shots. Controls the glass. |
| Stretch Big | Shooting + rebounding | Big who spaces the floor. Pick-and-pop. Forces bigs out. |
| Playmaking Big | Passing + IQ from the post | Big who creates for others. High-post facilitator. |
| Defensive Anchor | High defense + effort | Protects the rim. Every shot contested. The wall. |

**NO NBA player names anywhere in the app.** The archetypes stand alone as the player's OWN identity. "You're a Sharpshooter" is more empowering than "You're like [famous player]." If NBPA licensing is acquired later, famous example players can be added as optional references. This was an explicit legal compliance decision.

### Convergence Tracking

Each player's 9 attribute ratings form a radar chart. This shape is compared against the 8 archetype profiles using normalized cosine similarity. Result: "You're 78% Sharpshooter."

Multiple matches possible: "72% Sharpshooter, 65% Floor General" = a scoring point guard. Dominant archetype displayed prominently; secondary matches shown as "traits."

As the player trains and ratings change, convergence updates. AI explains the gap: "Your shooting matches the Sharpshooter profile. To close the gap: improve off-ball movement (current: 5, target: 7) and deep range consistency."

### Skill Trees (RPG-style, per skill)

Each of the 9 attributes has a 6-level progression. Cross-skill prerequisites force well-rounded development:

Example — Shooting:
- Lv 1: Form shooting (5ft, no defender)
- Lv 2: Mid-range pull-up (free throw to elbow)
- Lv 3: Three-point catch & shoot
- Lv 4: Three-point off screens (requires off-ball movement Lv 2)
- Lv 5: Pull-up three off the dribble (requires handles Lv 4)
- Lv 6: Deep range / contested (requires leg strength from Body Lab)

### XP + Engagement

**XP sources**: Complete drill (+10-25), Master play (+50), Practice session (+30), Watch/study play (+5), Game stats logged (+20).

**Streaks**: Consecutive days. Personal best displayed. One freeze per week.

**Badges**: Tied to real basketball accomplishments, not vanity — "Splash zone" (5 threes in a game), "Floor general" (10 assists), "Iron wall" (hold opponent under 30), "Play scholar" (master 10 plays), "Body work" (30 exercises), streak milestones.

### The Core Loop

Check archetype convergence → see skill gaps → open assigned drills → complete drills → earn XP → skill improves → convergence rises → new plays unlock → badges earned → open the app again tomorrow.
